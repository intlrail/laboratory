    International Rail provides a range of transportation solutions for daily commuters, businesses and casual travelers on three unique platforms: the Sonar Vacuum Transit (SVT), the Gravo-Sonar Vacuumt Transit (GSVT) and the Suborbital Ballistic Transit (SBT). All three networks are solely powered by ultrasonar radiation; the maintenance system is independently powered by geothermal heat and seismic waves.

    In addition to our scheduled itineraries, International Rail regularly offers free chartered operations for galactic research expeditions and frontline parcel services for the military personnel. If you are a member of the Science Academy and/or the Terran Armed Forces, you must be logged into your State ID account in order to access the booking service.

    SVT

    The Sonar Vacuum Transit (SVT) is the flagship operation and the bedrock of the International Rail enterprises. When the sonar-powered propulsion technology was first publicly introduced in 20*9, it was met with lukewarm reception. Following the closure of maglev-powered transport services in 2114, the SVT quickly filled in the void and grew into an indispensable part of our lives.

    GSVT

    The Gravity-Sonar Vacuum Transit (GSVT) is the current-generation technology built upon the existing framework of the SVT system. Today, the GSVT technology is behind nine out of ten service lines operated by the International Rail.

    SBT

    The Suborbital Ballistic Transit (SBT) is the next-generation aerotransport technology introduced by International Rail. As of 20*5, there are a total of 11 routes in daily service operation across all continents, departing every hour of the day and servicing more than 100,000 commuters around the clock. The SBT is our fastest-growing branch of the International Rail Network; at least eight new intracontinental routes are expected to launch by 2121.
